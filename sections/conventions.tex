\chapter*{Notation and Conventions for CES Aggregators}

\begin{calloutnote}[Purpose]
This chapter establishes notation for constant elasticity of substitution (CES) aggregators. The generalized mean is the master object; utility, production, risk, and intertemporal choice are special cases.
\end{calloutnote}

%----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section*{Summary of Notation}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection*{Aggregators}

Calligraphic letters denote aggregator functions:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}cll@{}}
\toprule
Symbol & Name & Domain \\
\midrule
$\M$ & Generalized mean (canonical) & Generic aggregation \\
$\Mbar$ & Generalized mean (classical) & Generic aggregation \\
$\Ssum$ & Inner sum & Pre-power aggregation \\
$\U$ & Utility & Consumption goods \\
$\F$ & Production & Factor inputs \\
$\Hfun$ & Output function & Allocation/distribution \\
$\CE$ & Certainty equivalent & Risky outcomes \\
$\V$ & Value function & Intertemporal utility \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection*{Parameters: Powers and Elasticities}

Each application uses a distinct elasticity. The power-elasticity relationship is:
\[
\text{power} = 1 - \frac{1}{\text{elasticity}}, \qquad \text{elasticity} = \frac{1}{1 - \text{power}}.
\]

\subsection*{Weights, Shares, and Probabilities}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}cll@{}}
\toprule
Symbol & Name & Properties \\
\midrule
$\omega_i$ & Primitive weight & $\omega_i > 0$, $\sum_i \omega_i = 1$ \\
$\tilde{\omega}_i$ & Transformed weight & After price/coordinate adjustment \\
$\wexp_i$ & Expenditure share & $\wexp_i = p_i c_i / W$ \\
$\pi_i$ & Probability & $\pi_i \geq 0$, $\sum_i \pi_i = 1$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection*{Vectors and Optima}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
Convention & Example \\
\midrule
Vectors in boldface & $\bx = (x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, $\bc$, $\bom$ \\
Sets in braces & $\{x_1, x_2\}$ \\
Optima with superscript $*$ & $c\opt$, $\U\opt$, $\Pstar$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{calloutcaution}[Notation Collision Avoided]
The asterisk $*$ is reserved for optimal values only. The classical form uses a bar: $\Mbar$, not $\M^*$.
\end{calloutcaution}
