
\chapter{Adjustment Costs and Risk: Shocks and Information}

This chapter extends the CES framework to environments with uncertainty and develops deep connections to information theory. We begin by introducing risk into the adjustment cost model, showing how the timing of information---whether shocks are realized before or after investment decisions---fundamentally changes the problem's structure. We then establish a ``grand isomorphism'' showing that price shocks, preference shocks, interest rate shocks, and productivity shocks are all mathematically equivalent in the CES framework.

The chapter concludes with speculative material connecting CES aggregation to Kullback-Leibler divergence, extreme value theory, and large deviations. These connections suggest that the CES structure may be more fundamental than its current economic applications indicate---it emerges naturally from information-theoretic principles and from the statistics of extreme outcomes.
